# Market Research

Use this skill for business, product, or go-to-market research. Keep recommendations grounded in evidence and separate estimates from facts.

## Workflow

1. Define the market:
   - product/category,
   - target customer,
   - geography,
   - timeframe,
   - price tier or segment.
2. Estimate opportunity:
   - TAM: total theoretical market,
   - SAM: reachable market for this product/category,
   - SOM: realistic near-term capture.
3. Explain methodology:
   - top-down source-based estimate,
   - bottom-up customer/count/price estimate,
   - proxy-based estimate if direct data is unavailable.
4. Identify trends:
   - growth drivers,
   - regulatory or platform changes,
   - technology shifts,
   - buyer behavior changes.
5. Map competition:
   - direct competitors,
   - substitutes,
   - open-source or DIY alternatives,
   - incumbent platforms.
6. Assess go-to-market:
   - customer segments,
   - channels,
   - positioning,
   - pricing signals,
   - adoption blockers.
7. Conclude with:
   - opportunity rating,
   - most promising segment,
   - key risks,
   - next validation steps.

## Output Shape

Use this structure unless the user asks otherwise:

1. Executive summary.
2. Market definition.
3. Market size and methodology.
4. Trends and demand drivers.
5. Competitive landscape.
6. Segments and channels.
7. Risks and assumptions.
8. Recommendation.
9. Sources.

## Quality Rules

- Never present estimates as exact facts.
- State assumptions explicitly.
- Prefer ranges when data is uncertain.
- Mention when data is old, regional, or proxy-based.
- Tie recommendations to evidence, not vibes.
