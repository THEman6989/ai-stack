# Competitor Analysis

Use this skill when the user wants to understand a competitive landscape or position a product against alternatives.

## Workflow

1. Define the comparison scope:
   - target market,
   - buyer/user,
   - geography,
   - product category,
   - evaluation criteria.
2. Identify competitors:
   - 3-5 direct competitors by default,
   - substitutes and DIY alternatives when relevant,
   - incumbents if they shape buyer expectations.
3. For each competitor, collect:
   - positioning,
   - target audience,
   - key features,
   - pricing model,
   - distribution/channel,
   - strengths,
   - weaknesses,
   - proof points or sources.
4. Build a comparison matrix.
5. Identify white-space opportunities:
   - underserved segment,
   - weaker competitor feature,
   - better pricing/package,
   - stronger workflow integration,
   - trust/security/performance angle.
6. Recommend positioning:
   - who to target first,
   - what to emphasize,
   - what battles to avoid.

## Output Shape

1. Summary.
2. Competitor set and why each was included.
3. Comparison matrix.
4. Competitor profiles.
5. Differentiation opportunities.
6. Recommended positioning.
7. Sources.

## Quality Rules

- Do not invent pricing or market share.
- Mark unknown data as unknown.
- Distinguish company claims from independent evidence.
- Include substitutes, not only obvious named competitors.
- Use citations close to claims.
